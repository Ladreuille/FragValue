FACEIT Season 8: the guide to start strong today

FACEIT CS2 Season 8 starts today, April 22, 2026. New Challenger thresholds per region, rewards, first 30-day strategy, 3-month plan. The guide to lock in your ELO from launch.

April 22, 2026 7 min read By FragValue

The 60-second summary

  • Season 8 starts today and runs about 3 months (until mid-July 2026).
  • Your ELO is not reset, but the Challenger ranking refreshes with the new season thresholds.
  • Estimated Challenger threshold per region: EU 3787, NA 3100, SA 2800, OCE and SEA 2500.
  • FACEIT Points (in-game currency rewards) reset to zero. Placement matches get a x1.5 boost ratio over the first 10 games.
  • The first 30 days are critical: that is when you set your baseline level for the season. Don't spam, play well.
The thing people often forget

The first 10 to 15 matches of the season weigh more than later ones. If you start poorly, you spend the rest of the quarter climbing back. Better to spend 2 hours warming up before your first ranked than queue cold and get placed below your real level.

What a FACEIT season is, exactly

A FACEIT season is a 3-month period during which FACEIT:

  1. Tracks your ranked matches (won, lost, ELO delta)
  2. Feeds the Challenger leaderboard of your region with your peak rank over the period
  3. Distributes FACEIT Points (FP) based on your performance, which you can spend in the shop (subscriptions, gear, tournaments)
  4. Resets the FPL Open slots: if you finish the season top 1000 EU, you get access to FPL Challenger pickups

Your ELO does not move between seasons. You keep your level and your ELO from one quarter to the next. What changes is your relative ranking (someone who climbed to 3800 ELO at the end of Season 7 might be just 120th EU on week 1 of Season 8 if many others also climbed).

Watch the dynamic curve

The Challenger threshold often climbs by 100 to 200 ELO over the season (more players climb, the top gets more contested). If you target Challenger, do not bet on "I am 150 ELO short by month 3": you will likely need 250+. Build a buffer.

FACEIT Points: what changes

FP is the FACEIT in-game currency, earned per match based on 3 criteria:

  • Match won: base of 25 to 75 FP depending on your ELO and your opponents'
  • Individual performance: 10 to 40 FP bonus if you are in the top 2 ratings of the match
  • Seasonal challenges: new set of 15 challenges in Season 8, rewards between 500 and 5000 FP

FP resets to zero each new season. If you accumulated 8000 unspent FP in Season 7, they are spendable until April 29 before they expire. Check your shop.

First 30-day strategy

The most important window of your season. Here is how to approach it.

Week 1: warm-up and implicit placement matches

FACEIT does not have official "placement matches", but the first 10 matches of the season carry more weight in the ELO adjustment algo (mechanism not documented but observed by the community).

Week 1 goals:

  • 45-min minimum warm-up before each session (aim lab + deathmatch)
  • Queue only in optimal conditions: no matches when tired or after a long evening
  • Focus on maps you are solid on (don't test Anubis if you have played it 3 times)
  • Stable duo or trio recommended if possible, to stabilize performance

Weeks 2-3: consolidation

You have a first ELO benchmark. Now it is about consolidating without regressing.

  • Aim for a WR of 55%+ over 20 matches. If you fall below 50%, stop and analyze.
  • Test new maps progressively (1 new map per week max).
  • Do one demo review per week on a match lost while having the advantage (see our 30-min method).

Week 4: first review

You have around 30 matches behind you. Compare:

  • Your end-of-month-1 ELO vs your start-of-season ELO
  • Your Season 8 FV Rating vs your Season 7 FV Rating (on the FragValue dashboard)
  • Your maps with WR > 55% vs those at < 45%: you already know what to pick and ban in veto
Realistic month 1 goal

For a level 8-9, targeting +100 to +250 ELO over the first 30 days is realistic. For a low level 10, +80 to +200 ELO. Beyond that, you are betting on an exceptional run rather than steady progress.

Total volume: 78 to 107 matches over the season. That is 6 to 8 matches per week on average. Don't fall into the farming trap: 20 matches a week without analysis equals stagnation.

Classic season-start mistakes

  • Queueing on launch day with no warm-up. Everyone is hyped, the average level is close to your rating. You start poorly, you panic.
  • Changing your setup "for the new season". Stability first. If you want a new crosshair or sensitivity, do it at the end of the previous season.
  • Testing new maps in ranked. The first 10 matches are critical, stick to your safe maps. Tests go in deathmatch or wingman.
  • Spamming 6 matches in a row. After 3 matches, your decision quality drops by 15 to 20%. You lose ELO you'll have to climb back.
  • Underestimating unspent FP. Use your Season 7 8000 FP before April 29 or you lose them.
