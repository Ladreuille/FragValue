ADR, KAST, FV Rating: the 3 stats that actually tell your level

K/D is misleading. ADR, KAST and FV Rating measure your real impact in the team. We explain what each metric means, how it is calculated, and the thresholds for each FACEIT level.

April 22, 2026 7 min read By FragValue

Why K/D is not enough

K/D (kills divided by deaths) is the most-watched and most-misleading stat. Concrete example: a player with 25/15 on a map lost 16-11 can sit at a 1.67 K/D, while his teammate at 18/13 with 95 ADR and 82% KAST was much more useful in the effort to win rounds.

K/D says nothing about:

  • When you got your kills (an eco kill and a decisive kill count the same)
  • The impact of your deaths (dying early in opening costs more than a save)
  • The damage piled up that didn't end in a kill (and that helped your teammates)
  • Your contribution on rounds where you got zero kills (assist, flash, trade)

The 3 stats below each fix a blind spot of K/D.

1. ADR (Average Damage per Round)

ADR = total damage / number of rounds. It is the most direct stat to measure your offensive contribution, whether you finish your kills or not.

Why it matters: in CS2, a kill = 100 HP minimum. But dealing 180 damage spread across 2 enemies (one at 100 HP = kill, the other at 80 HP = pre-damaged) is often more useful for the team than a single clean kill. Your teammate finishes the second one without risk.

How it is calculated

Every bullet that hits counts as damage (capped at 100 HP per enemy per round, beyond is overkill that doesn't count). FACEIT sums the damage from all your shots over rounds played, then divides by round count.

2. KAST (Kills, Assists, Survived, Traded %)

KAST measures the percentage of rounds where you had at least one positive contribution: a kill, an assist, surviving, or being traded (your death avenged within 5 seconds).

It is the best metric to measure your steady impact in the team. A player with 40 kills but 60% KAST = he carries a few rounds and is absent on the others. A player with 20 kills but 80% KAST = he is useful on almost every round. KAST is also one of the main factors of the consistency component of the FV Rating (25% of the final score).

How it is calculated

Each round, FACEIT checks:

  • Kill: at least 1 kill?
  • Assist: substantial damage on a teammate kill, or flash assist?
  • Survived: alive at round end?
  • Traded: died but a teammate traded your death within 5s?

If at least one of the 4 is true, you count for KAST that round. KAST % = rounds with at least 1 check / total rounds.

The trap to avoid

A KAST that is too high (> 80% over a long period) with low ADR can be a signal of passive play. You survive a lot, you tap little. That is useful in support roles but a problem in entry. Both stats must be read together.

Rule of thumb

If KAST > 75% and ADR < 75: you play too safe, take more initiative.

If ADR > 85 and KAST < 65%: you put up flashes of brilliance but you die too early. Work on post-kill positioning.

3. FV Rating: our composite metric

FV Rating is the FragValue equivalent of HLTV Rating 2.1. It is a composite indicator that weights several inputs to reflect your overall impact.

What it contains

FV Rating combines 4 weighted factors:

  1. Per-round impact (kills, damage, multi-kills): 40%
  2. Consistency (KAST, frequency of zero rounds): 25%
  3. Clutch factor (1vX played and won): 20%
  4. Side balance (CT vs T performance): 15%

We explain the full formula in this dedicated article. The number typically sits between 0.70 and 1.30.

Difference vs HLTV Rating 2.1

HLTV Rating 2.1 is calculated on pro matches (LAN, online tier 1-2). It does not factor in your FACEIT play. FV Rating is specific to your FACEIT history and accounts for the skill of your opponents (a kill against a level 10 weighs more than a kill against a level 3).

How to use these 3 stats together

The real value is reading them in combination. Here are 4 typical profiles these 3 stats reveal:

Profile "Offensive carry"

FV Rating 1.10+, ADR 90+, KAST 70%. You make the difference when you take your duels, but you have ghost rounds. Good for open-up and entry roles.

Profile "Steady impact"

FV Rating 1.05+, ADR 80+, KAST 75%+. The ideal profile. You are present each round and you put up damage. Everyone wants this player on their team.

Profile "Passive support"

FV Rating 0.95, ADR 70, KAST 80%+. You survive well, you trade, you help, but you do little direct damage. Useful as pure support (IGL, flash), limited elsewhere.

Profile "Inconsistent"

FV Rating 0.85, ADR 70, KAST 62%. High variance. You play matches at 1.20 and matches at 0.50. Consistency problem, possibly tilt. Mental focus.
