FV Rating explained: how we calculate your real CS2 impact

FV Rating combines 4 weighted factors: per-round impact, consistency, clutch factor and side balance. We open the full formula, explain why HLTV Rating 2.1 isn't always the best benchmark for a FACEIT player, and how to read your score.

April 22, 2026 6 min read By FragValue

Why we built the FV Rating

HLTV Rating 2.1 has become the standard for measuring a pro player's impact. But it is calibrated on pro matches (tier 1-2 LAN and online), with a very specific context: coordinated 5v5, called strats, coordinated utility.

For a FACEIT player in solo queue, that context does not exist. You play with randoms, you are the IGL (or nobody is), strats are improvised. The rating must reflect impact in this context, not extrapolate from pro matches.

So we designed FV Rating for your FACEIT environment, with 4 components that each measure a different aspect of your contribution to the team.

The 4 weighted components

The complete formula:

FV Rating = weighted average

40% Per-round impact + 25% Consistency + 20% Clutch factor + 15% Side balance

Centered on 1.00 (average player at your level), typically between 0.70 and 1.30.

Component 1: Per-round impact (40%)

The "offensive" part: how much value you generate each round.

Sub-formula:

  • Kills per round (KPR) normalized vs level
  • Damage per round (ADR) normalized vs level
  • Multi-kills (2k, 3k, 4k, 5k) weighted non-linearly: a 3k counts more than three single kills
  • Opening duels won (first kill of the round)

Why 40%: it is the most tangible component. You generate damage or you don't. But it is not 100% because making kills without context (eco fragging, padding) does not measure the difference at high level. To understand the raw stats that feed this factor, check the complete CS2 metrics guide where each KPI is broken down (KPR, ADR, opening duels, multi-kills).

Component 2: Consistency (25%)

The "reliability" part: are you present every round?

Sub-formula:

  • KAST % (Kills / Assists / Survived / Traded)
  • Standard deviation of your stats over 20 matches (lower is better)
  • Ghost rounds (0 kill + 0 assist + early death): penalty if > 4 per match
  • Frequency of matches with < 0.80 individual FV Rating (your "off days")

Why 25%: an inconsistent carry is less useful than a regular player with moderate impact. The matches you lose are the ones where you are absent, not the ones where you don't carry. This consistency gap also shows up in our article on ADR and KAST, which explains why KAST distinguishes truly steady players from volatile carries.

Component 3: Clutch factor (20%)

The "pressure" part: your performance in moments that matter.

Sub-formula:

  • 1vX win rates (1v1, 1v2, 1v3+) weighted
  • Decisive rounds (close score, key economy rounds) win rate
  • Overtime performance (where applicable)
  • Clutch when bomb is planted (retake vs defend)

Why 20%: decisive rounds are rare but disproportionately important. Winning one 1v2 clutch often shifts the momentum of an entire half. We normalized vs the minimum number of rounds (if you played 0 clutches, the factor is neutral, not penalized).

Component 4: Side balance (15%)

The "completeness" part: are you good on CT AND on T, or only on one side?

Sub-formula:

  • CT Win Rate vs T Win Rate: bonus if both are decent, penalty if either is < 40%
  • Role diversity: do you play entry, lurker, support depending on the round?

Why 15%: a 70% CT / 30% T player is less valuable than a 55% / 55% one. You can't pick only the sides where you shine.

Important to understand

The FV Rating is normalized vs your level. A 1.05 at level 5 means "you are above the level 5 average". You will get a different rating with the same stats at level 10 (because the bar is higher).

That is why FV Rating doesn't directly compare a level 5 to a level 10: it compares each one to its own benchmark. If you aim to climb levels, read our FACEIT level-by-level roadmap which details expectations per tier and the KPIs to target.

Why we don't copy HLTV Rating 2.1

HLTV Rating 2.1 is a great metric, but it was calibrated for:

  • Pro LAN and online tier 1-2 matches: very different context from FACEIT solo
  • Coordinated utility: pros have 4 teammates setting up
  • Strict competitive maps: reduced map pool, no Dust2 or Vertigo pickup

If we apply HLTV Rating 2.1 to your FACEIT play, you get a number that underestimates your clutches (you take more in solo queue) and overestimates your damage (no organized trade frags).

We ran internal tests: across 10k FACEIT profiles analyzed, HLTV Rating 2.1 and FV Rating correlate at 0.82 (strong correlation, but not identical). The 18% divergence is concentrated on "solo carry" and "silent support" profiles, exactly the profiles HLTV Rating reads poorly.

How to use your FV Rating

1. Compare it to your own history

A given month's FV Rating isn't interesting in absolute terms. What matters is your trend. 30d at 0.92, 90d at 0.87? You are progressing. It is dropping? You are regressing, analyze what changed.

2. Compare it to your teammates

On the compare page, you can put 2 to 5 profiles side by side. If you play with someone at 1.05 and you sit at 0.90, the question is: what does he do that you don't?

3. Compare it to your opponents

Before an important match or a scrim, check the FV Rating of opposing players on the Scout. You can anticipate where their carry will be (high ADR, high HS%).

4. Check your components

On the dashboard, we give you the breakdown of the 4 components. If your FV Rating sits at 1.00 with "Impact 1.10, Consistency 0.85, Clutch 1.05, Side 0.95", your real gap is consistency. You have matches where you disappear.

Known limitations

Honest disclaimer:

  • FV Rating is based on your last 20 matches. If you played 19 solo and 1 stack match, the stack weighs heavily.
  • It does not account for your team composition (a level 10 with 4 level 5 teammates will see a deflated FV Rating because teammates lose rounds).
  • It does not yet integrate comms or qualitative game sense (we are working on that via AI demo analysis).
